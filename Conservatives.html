Conservative Party

Who are they?

The Conservative party is a political party founded in 1834. It is the oldest political party in the United Kingdom and have famously been led by prime ministers Winston Churchill and Margaret Thatcher. As of 2019 they are currently in office being led by prime minister Theresa May. The Conservative party has a plan to move “forward together” to create a “stronger Britain”.

What are their main policies?

The main policies of the Conservative political party can be split up into multiple sections:

NHS and healthcare

No one with less than £100,000 in assets will have to pay for care
But at-home care will be means-tested
Care payments from the value of a family home will be deferred
NHS spending 'will rise £8bn in real terms'
Live online A&E waiting time information
10,000 mental health professionals will be recruited

Economy and finance

The deficit will be wiped out by "the middle of the next decade"
National Living Wage for over-25s
Tax-free personal allowance will rise to £12,500
40p tax threshold will rise to £50,000
Corporation tax will fall to 17%
VAT will not be increased
But National Insurance could rise, it just isn't spelt out
Long-term reforms
Tax 'advisory firms' that aid avoidance will face tougher regulation

Education and childcare

£4billion fund by 2022 will stop schools losing cash
At least 100 new free schools a year
All new primary schools should include a nursery
Councils will be banned from creating new places in schools on the two lowest Ofsted ratings
No cut to tuition fees.
New teachers will not have to repay student loans while they remain the profession
New-style 'T-levels' will replace 13,000 technical qualifications
All 11-year-olds must know times tables off by heart
Apprentices will get 'significantly discounted' bus and train travel

Housing and welfare

A million homes between 2015 and 2020 in total, and another 500,000 by 2022
Compulsory purchase will be reformed, making it easier for councils to buy up derelict land
A promise of new social housing
Pledge to halve rough sleeping by 2022 and 'eliminate it altogether' by 2027
Reduce levels of child poverty
No plans to reverse Tory welfare cuts
Employers will get a 12-month holiday on paying National Insurance if they hire ex-offenders or people who've been unemployed for a year A million more disabled people 'will get into work' over 10 years

Defence

Trident nuclear weapons programme will be renewed
Britain will commit to Nato target of spending 2% of GDP on defence - and increase spending by 0.5% above inflation
0.7% of GDP will be spent on foreign aid
British troops will not be subject to the European Court of Human Rights
Firms hiring armed forces veterans will get a one-year holiday from paying employers' National Insurance
Social media firms will be forced to delete historic records of users from before they turned 18
Penalties will be 'threatened' against social media firms that fail to remove or avoid posting hate speech or harmful content
An "independent public advocate" will act for families in disasters like Hillsborough
£1billion will be invested to create 10,000 prison places
A new Domestic Violence and Abuse Bill will bring new protection for victims

Brexit

A Trade Bill will be introduced and new UK schedules lodged with the World Trade Organisation
A Shared Prosperity Fund will redistribute 'money coming back as we leave the EU' to the four nations
The Common Travel Area will be maintained
Workers' rights conferred by EU law will be maintained
A Great Repeal Bill will transfer EU law into UK law to then be altered or discarded

Environment

Fracking wont be banned
Planning law will be changed to 'permit' non-fracking drilling by fracking firms
A larger percentage of tax from fracking will go to local communities under the Shale Wealth Fund
£600million will be ploughed into making 'almost' every car and van zero-emission by 2050
A million trees will be planted in towns and cities

Immigration

Net immigration will be cut below 100,000 a year
Students will still be kept in net migration figures
The immigration skills charge will be doubled
The Immigration Health Surcharge for foreigners using the NHS will rise
The Migration Advisory Committee will be asked to recommend on a new visa strategy
They will 'work to reduce' asylum claims by refugees instead of committing to take more of them
